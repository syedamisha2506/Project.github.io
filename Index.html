Nobel Prizes

Between 1901 and 1901, the Nobel Prizes and the Sveriges Riksbank Prize in Economic Sciences in Memory of Alfred Nobel were awarded 629 times to 1,018 people and organisations. The Nobel Prize is an international award administered by the Nobel Foundation in Stockholm, Sweden, and based on the fortune of Alfred Nobel, Swedish inventor and entrepreneur. In 1968, Sveriges Riksbank established the Sveriges Riksbank Prize in Economic Sciences in Memory of Alfred Nobel, founder of the Nobel Prize. Each prize consists of a medal, a personal diploma, and a cash award.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Nobel Prize?

The Nobel Prize is a set of annual international awards in recognition of academic, cultural, or scientific advances.

Who founded the Nobel Prize?

The prizes were established by Alfred Nobel, a Swedish industrialist, inventor, and philanthropist.

When were the first Nobel Prizes awarded?

The first Nobel Prizes were awarded in 1901.

Which fields are covered by the Nobel Prize?

Physics, Chemistry, Medicine, Literature, Peace, and Economic Sciences.

How are Nobel Laureates selected?

They are chosen by various committees and organizations responsible for each category.

Where are the Nobel Prizes awarded?

The Peace Prize is awarded in Oslo, Norway; the others are awarded in Stockholm, Sweden.
